A New Community at UC Davis

After our first year at UC Davis, a few friends and I felt the university lacked a strong computer science community. And with our largely theoretical coursework, it was difficult to learn how to apply our skills. In September of 2020, we decided to start our own software development organization. CodeLab is an organization where undergraduate developers and designers work on projects with actual industry clients. Students get the opportunity to have hands-on industry experience and also be part of a tight-knit social and professional community.

My Role

In addition to development roles (learn more here), I serve as the Vice President of External for CodeLab. I handle all communications for the organization, including social media, written article content, our website, and the design of many marketing materials.

Logo

As a new organization, we needed a logo! After playing around with various designs, we selected the design below. The dot-slash refers to the syntax for the current working directory, and thus the syntax used to execute a compiled script in a UNIX environment.

Website

In Summer 2021, I redesigned the website, which is one of my favorite wireframes I've created to date. In Fall 2021, I led and mentored a student team to develop the site in React. In addition to teaching the students frontend development, I planned and delegated tasks over 6 weeks of Agile development. It was incredible seeing my team grow their development and communication skills throughout the quarter, and I am so proud to see what they were able to create. The website is currently live at codelabdavis.com.
